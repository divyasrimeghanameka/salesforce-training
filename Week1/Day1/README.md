## What is Salesforce?

Salesforce is a cloud-based CRM platform used to manage customers, sales, service, and marketing in one place.

---

## What is CRM?

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management.

It helps companies manage customer interactions and business relationships.

---

## Why Companies Choose Salesforce?

Salesforce stores customer data in one place and automates many business tasks.

It is:
- Cloud-based
- Easy to access
- Customizable for business needs

---

## Why CRM Gives ROI?

CRM:
- Improves sales
- Saves employee time through automation
- Helps companies retain customers
- Supports better decision-making using reports and analytics

---

## Dashboard

Dashboard is the main screen where users can view:
- Records
- Tasks
- Reports
- Recent activities in Salesforce

---

## Leads

Leads are potential customers who may be interested in buying a company’s product or service.

---

## Accounts

Accounts store details about companies or organizations related to the business.

---

## Contacts

Contacts are people associated with an account or company.

---

## Opportunities

Opportunities are possible sales deals that are tracked by the sales team.

---

## What are Objects in Salesforce?

Objects are like database tables used to store a particular type of information in Salesforce.

---

## What are Records in Salesforce?

A record is a single entry stored inside an object.

Example:
One customer detail inside the Contact object.

# Core Task (MANDATORY)

## Task 1: Understand Business Flow

Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer

- A Lead is a person who shows interest in a product or service.
- When the company gets proper details and communication starts, the Lead becomes a Contact.
- If there is a chance of making a sale, it is called an Opportunity.
- When the deal is successfully completed, the person or company becomes a Customer.

| Salesforce Term | Real-Life Mapping |
